Synagogue Etiquette

Some of you are very familiar with a synagogue service like ours, and to others it is all new. For those who are interested, here are a few tips on what will happen during Sam’s bar mitzvah service.

The synagogue is at 1180 University Drive, NE, in Atlanta. There is a parking lot, and parking is permitted on the street as well. People start to arrive for the Saturday morning services at 9:00, and the services end around 1:00, including kiddush and lunch. It is a long service with many different parts – it will give you a sense of how much work Sam has done to prepare for this day! Sam will begin his part (leading the Torah service) around 9:45. You are welcome to sit anywhere you like.

Order of the Service (times are approximate):
9:00      Psukei D'Zimrah ("hymnal verses")
9:30      Shacharit service
9:45      Torah service
              Sam’s D'var Torah (sermon)
              Haftarah
              Rabbi's D'var Torah
              Rest of the Torah service
11:00    Musaf service
              Presentations, blessings, and conclusion of the service
1:00      Kiddush blessings
              Kiddush and lunch

During the service, you should feel free to participate in any way that feels comfortable to you – sing (it's fine if you don't know the words or the tune!) or hum along, read the prayer book, meditate, or just observe a way of worship that may be new to you.  It is also fine to bring your own book to read. If you want to take a short break during the service, you can; you’re welcome to go outside or to the playground area for a few minutes. Make sure to come back in for the end of the service! We only ask you not to walk in or out of the sanctuary while Sam or the Rabbi is speaking.

Cell phones and other electronics are not allowed in the synagogue and we encourage you to leave them at home. If you need to call home, you may do so outside. We don’t allow writing or drawing in the synagogue on Shabbat, and there will be no picture-taking or recording. Our tradition is to respect the day by focusing on the service and our own meditations.

What to wear? The dress code is "business casual." The building is air-conditioned so you may want to bring a light sweater or jacket.

Wearing kippot (head coverings) is customary for boys and men, and girls and women can wear one if they choose. Wearing a kippah is done as a sign of respect, like removing your hat in church, or taking off shoes at a mosque.  Although at some b'nai mitzvah people give out kippot, we have chosen not to do so, since most bar mitzvah-goers already have large kippah collections!  We will have some kippot available for you to borrow for the service and kiddush (reception) afterwards – please bring your own favorite kippah if you have one. Many Jewish people will also wear a tallit (prayer shawl), which is a reminder of Jewish commandments and tradition, but this is optional.

Shortly after noon, the service will end and we will have lunch. We will ask you to wait until the prayer is said over the wine/grape juice and the bread. Then you should get a plate and help yourself at the buffet, and please sit at any of the tables. Feel free to make new friends!

Thank you so much for respecting our customs, and for celebrating with us. Please let us know if you have questions or concerns.

We look forward to sharing this celebration with you!
